R&D Channels
im+m, inc.
instructional media + magic, inc. develops standards-based multimedia courseware for colleges and universities, and the supporting information technology infrastructure. Based on research from early initiatives, im+m developed multimedia-rich classroom presentations and on-line courses leading to the improvement of student retention and performance.
Partnering with faculty, im+m leverages media and course-development expertise to the process of authoring standards-based courseware. Focusing on undergraduate courses, im+m combines access to extensive copyright-free federal materials and in house production of instructional audio and video together with faculty authored course materials to create media-rich on-line interactive courseware.

im+m Current Projects
A list of the current projects under development at im+m.
CREE - Contextual Resource Evaluation Environment
The University of Hull and its partners, the Universities of Oxford, Edinburgh, and York, and Newark and Sherwood College, are improving the productivity of search and information resource interfaces used by researchers and students, and portal functionality and integration. im+m provides technical support and liaison. im+m is developing prototype JSR 168 and WSRP portlets supporting user preferences, and working with the U.S. Library of Congress on the implementation of Web Services based exchange of search data.
PESC SunGard SCT e-Authentication Update
im+m provided assistance in organizing and documenting the August 2004 update on e-Authentication standards and their implementation in higher education. im+m's Jon Allen produced the DVD of the event now being distributed by SunGard SCT to the higher education community.
SAKAI
JA-SIG Java Administration Special Interest Group
im+m is participated as chief designer in the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation-funded support of the development of JA-SIG's uPortal 2.3.3. This work continues with implementation and enhancements in forthcoming version 3.0. im+m also provides project administration for the uPortal project.

Public Funding book reports on costs and benefits of education technology

"Public Funding of Higher Education," a Johns Hopkins University book edited by Sigma Group Director Ed St. John and Michael D. Parsons, reviews costs and outcomes of instructional technology and distance education. Based on Justin Tilton and Jim Farmer's work in Arizona, the chapter cites improved student outcomes at Yavapia, Rio Salado, and Northland Pioneer Colleges. The chapter also provides cost data from the Arizona community colleges and universities. Monitoring class size and implementing standards makes education technology cost-effective.
