Its in the best interest of all concerned to develop systems and sites that do not hinder users with disabilities from accessing information. In addition, many of the guidelines are fundamental to good usability and therefore are a benefit to all users regardless of ability. It is also important to provide a system that institutions can deploy with as little customization as possible.

Kuali intends to achieve conformance level ‘A’ based on the guidelines of the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines. This conformance level is achieved by complying with all priority level 1 checkpoints. Additionally, Kuali has chosen to follow many of the priority level 2 checkpoints.

Kuali has prepared a complete document outlining it's Accessibility Guidelines. This document lists the priority 1 and priority 2 checkpoints with a comparison to Section 508. The website will be updated periodically to outline the specific priority level 1 and 2 checkpoints that are being met in the development life cycle.
